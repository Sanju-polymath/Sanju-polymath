About
Sanjay
Bharadwaj
Stage · Production · Spatial Design

Ten years working where engineering meets live performance — as stage manager, production manager, festival manager, set designer, and spatial designer, often wearing several of these hats on the same project.

A defining strand of the practice is product design for performance: sets, props, and technical systems designed to be built within budget, toured across cities, and reused show after show.

Over the past two years the practice has expanded into self-taught 3D modelling and auditorium design, and into producing new media art installations — bringing the same rigour to technologically ambitious, immersive work.

Ranga Shankara  ·  QTP Entertainment  ·  British Council
Stage Manager · Production Manager · Festival Manager · 2015 – present
Beginning as a freelance stage manager in Bangalore before joining the in-house repertory at Ranga Shankara — over seven productions and more than 200 shows, managing the complete lifecycle of each production from rehearsal room to touring stage. Ronak & Jessie
BookMyShow Live  ·  Dir. Feroz Abbas Khan
Technical Coordinator · Mumbai → Delhi Tour
A large-scale Punjabi musical inspired by Romeo and Juliet, produced by BookMyShow Live and directed by Feroz Abbas Khan. As Technical Coordinator, I managed the complete transportation of the production from Mumbai to JLN Auditorium, Delhi — nine container trucks of sets, trussing, lighting, costumes, and backstage infrastructure. On-site I constructed independent truss systems, adapted the stage environment against the venue's structural limitations, oversaw on-site refabrication where set pieces could not fit the loading bays, and supervised full dismantling and return logistics after the run.

Bliss Circus
Open-Air Jungle Circus  ·  Goa
Stage / Residency Manager · 2022
Residency Manager and Stage Manager at Bliss Circus, a coastal open-air jungle circus in Goa. Underwent aerial rigging training and developed full maintenance routines for circus rigs, wooden stages, costumes, and apparatus exposed to salt air, humidity, and monsoon. Devised natural termite prevention systems for wooden structures, vacuum-sealed and drum-stored costumes against mould, and inspected rigging weekly for coastal wear — learning firsthand how nature, architecture, and live performance coexist. Lived in a treehouse opposite the stage for the duration of the residency.

Echoes of Earth
India's Leading Sustainable Arts & Music Festival
Installation Artist · Production · 2024
Worked as installation artist and production contributor at Echoes of Earth, one of India's leading sustainable arts and music festivals. Upcycled waste materials into large-scale sculpture — including a wasp sculpture built entirely from collected waste — while also prototyping light installations and fabricating electronics housings for the festival's new media artworks. Combined hands-on fabrication, technical problem-solving, and a commitment to sustainable material practice throughout.

Auditorium Design
Prestige Centre for Performing Arts  ·  Idagunji Mahaganapati Yakshagana Mandali
Auditorium Designer · Self-Taught 3D Modelling · 2025
Self-taught 3D modelling entirely from a practical need: to design performance spaces differently from the resource-heavy, inflexible auditoriums I had spent a decade working inside. The resulting concepts explore modular seating, dynamic stage orientations, DC-powered lighting infrastructure with centralised DMX control, and lightweight geodesic structures. These visualisations led directly to commissions — the conversion of a banquet hall at Prestige Centre for Performing Arts into a flexible black-box venue, and 3D visualisation work for Idagunji Mahaganapati Yakshagana Mandali, in collaboration with Prakash Belawadi.

Sensistan — Circle 3 Residency

Residency Mentor — Production Design · 2024
Core team member at Circle 3 Residency (Sensistan), a new media art residency using TouchDesigner and projection mapping. Stories in Half — Set Design
Theatre Production  ·  Loom Set Construction
Set Designer · Production · Loom Construction
Set design and construction for Stories in Half — a site-specific theatrical production centred on a large-scale handbuilt loom as the primary scenic element. The loom was designed, fabricated, and rigged as a structural and aesthetic centrepiece, documented across the full build process from raw materials to final stage configuration.
