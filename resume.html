Profile

Ten years across theatre, live performance, festivals, residencies, and installations — beginning with backstage work at Ranga Shankara and touring the country with productions for QTP Entertainment and numerous private organisations.

My practice bridges the precision of engineering with the demands of live performance. A defining strand is the lifecycle of a performance artefact: designing sets and props that fold down, pack efficiently for road and air transport, survive long-term storage, and reassemble on stage looking exactly as they should — with no trace of the journey they have made. Getting that right requires thinking simultaneously as a designer, an engineer, and a logistician.

Alongside this, I work across stage management, production management, festival management, set design, and new media installation production — often several of these on the same project.

Education & Formation

I flunked high school and do not hold a formal educational degree. My subjects in 12th grade were Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics and Electronics — though I used to fail in written examinations due to a learning disability, dyslexia. In practicals and live experimentation, however, I always used to ace it: building and making things in the real world came naturally where written tests did not. I have been active in theatre since childhood — it kept me occupied and grounded, and most of what I know has been learned in rehearsal halls, on stage, and on site, from the time I was a child.

Khoj Fellowship  —  Swaraj University, Udaipur 2016 – 2017
Scholarship recipient  ·  Self-Designed Learning Programme

Swaraj University is a two-year experimental learning fellowship for ages 18–28, built around self-designed education. Rather than a fixed curriculum, each Khoji designs their own learning path around genuine curiosity — drawing from a network of over 1,000 mentors across India: artisans, ecologists, designers, farmers, filmmakers, activists and more. The programme emphasises self-awareness, skill-building, unlearning institutional conditioning, and sustainable community living. There are no degrees — the outcome is a learning portfolio and the discipline of knowing how to direct your own growth. This fellowship deepened the way I approach every project: with rigour, improvisation, and a willingness to go where the work demands.
